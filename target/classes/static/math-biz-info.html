Program Summary Earn two degrees in five years in both computer science and business by enrolling in this unique program. Receive a full Bachelor of Computer Science from Waterloo and a full Bachelor of Business Administration from Wilfrid Laurier University.

One other 4U course

Additional Requirements An Admission Information Form is required. We will send you instructions when we acknowledge your application. You're strongly encouraged to write the Euclid Mathematics Contest and the Canadian Computing Competition.

Clubs There are about 185 clubs on the Waterloo campus, and they offer an intramural and extramural program that you can join with your friends, or registering on your own and being assigned a team. At Wilfrid Laurier the intramural program is award winning and half the school attends, with many activities ranging from water polo to dodgeball. There are over 200 clubs at U Waterloo, and if there isn’t a specific course that piques your interest, than you can create your own with the help of WUSA (Waterloo Undergraduate Student Association “Run by students for students”) aside from these clubs, waterloo also offers many on campus activities such as Orientation, Welcome Week, and other events run by WUSA and student societies, sports teams you can join or cheer on, concerts by our campus choirs, a cappella groups, and orchestra and many more.
Courses Courses you will take can include Social Entrepreneurship, Business Strategy for Sustainability, Business Law, High-Tech Marketing, Transportation and Facilities Management, Management of Financial Institutions, Strategic Human Resources Planning as well as many more. In this program you can also choose an area of focus, with choices such as Business and Sustainability, Entrepreneurship, Finance, Human Resource Management, Insurance and Risk Management, International Business and many more. Courses you will take: from September to December, CS 135 - Designing Functional Programs, MATH 135 - Algebra, MATH 137 - Calculus 1, One communication skills course and One elective. From January to April CS 136 - Algorithm Design and Data Abstraction, MATH 136 - Linear Algebra 1, MATH 138 - Calculus 2, as well as Two electives. After the first year you will take mostly computer science related courses, and if you would like you can decide to minor in various other subjects.
