Business Management

University University of Toronto
Degree Bachelor of Business Administration/BBA
OUAC Program Code TSM
Grade Range high 80s
Program Summary In this program you will receive the BBA or the bachelor of business administration degree, the program length is 4 years, and there are currently 2 campuses where you can attend this course: Mississauga campus, and Scarbrough campus. (keep in mind that the 2 campuses have different requirements.) The Mississauga campus is located 3359 Mississauga Rd, Mississauga, and the Scarborough campus is located at 1265 Military Trail, Scarborough. In the scarbrough campus they solely offer co-op programs, however the mississauga campus does not.
Prerequisites Required courses:
Ontario Secondary School Diploma Six 4U/M courses, including:

Advanced Functions (MHF4U)

English (ENG4U)
Tuition Around $16,000 (housing, food etc... excluded)
Ranking U of T is ranked 1st in Canada as of 2021
Job Advertising Manager
additional info 46k - 140k salary Their job is to pique the interests of potential buyers for an organisation, company etc. they work in advertising agencies, create campaigns, and also work in the finance department to organise costs.
Job Auditor
additional info 39k - 124k salary They go over financial records, and make sure that all payments are paid on time, and that there is no mismanagement of finances.
Job economist
additional info 53k - 133k salary Apply economic knowledge to various fields, some examine employment levels, business cycles etc. and some analyse the effects of taxes, inflation etc.
Clubs There are roughly 150 clubs at the mississauga campus, and almost 200 in the scarborough campus. There are a wide variety of clubs, which vary differently depending on which campus you choose.
Courses There are 21 courses you can take in this program, some of which include advanced facilities management, business strategy, business management, understanding financial statements, and many more.
Employment/Passing rates Passing rate is 79.3%, and employment rate after 6 months is 84.4%%, and after 2 years is 95.7%, (all stats as of 2016.)
